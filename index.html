About Me

My Background

   I have been programming for a little over the past 4 years. I was going to be a Bio/Stats major, but it all changed when on a whim to take an "Intro to C++" course with a friend.

   Turns out, this would be a turning point in my education. No longer was I happy with knowing the material, instead I was determined to understand it. From that point on, of the 9 eligible semesters/quarters, I was on the Dean's List for 8 of them.

   Unfortunately for me, this intense focus on learning and education blinded me to something that is quite important in this industry: Internships. In retrospect, this was something I should've pursued during my time at UC Irvine; however, I am proud of how I earned my degree and latin honors.

   So instead of those, I focused on my education and personal projects, naively thinking they were equal. But even though that they are not considered to be as important, they were still learning experiences. From start to finish of each one, I discovered and understood something new.

   I realized something that should seem quite obvious. I don't know everything. I was faced with some difficult projects and challenges. It wasn't immediately clear as to which was the ideal approach towards an optimal solution. These tough problems required me to identify the logic, consult peers, and/or reference the language documentation. I may not know everything, but I am willing to learn.

My Interests

   One of the largest non-computer related interests of mine is Soccer. My roles of the sport has changed quite a bit over the years, but I have managed to try a bit of everything. I have played for ~16 years, refereed for ~4 years, coached for 1 year, and trained for 2 years. This is something that i have managed to always enjoy, regardless of my age.

   Something that is definitely not unique to me, is that I enjoy playing video games. I mainly play games on PC, but a select few on Xbox One. Video gaming is important to me, since through it I am able to maintain and enrich long-distance relationships. Besides that, generally they are quite fun to play, whether that is due to the game mechanics or the socialization varies from game to game.

My Objectives

   I would consider myself an adept programmer, given my grades and enjoyment at it. But there is always room for improvement. As selfish as it sounds this is one of my objectives: I want to code more efficiently and intelligently.

   At UC Irvine, I had a variety of projects varying in difficulty. The most satisfying experience was to finally run the solution with no errors in the unit cases. But it wasn't great because I was done with the problem. It was because I understood the solution. It was that I had been given a difficult task, and with time and resources, I was able to find the solution. I want to be given a problem, and understand the solution to it.

   I worked with many groups on a variety of different projects. With members all varying in skill, but regardless still approached development in a faux-agile style. We would meet most of the week, and during these meetings we would cover planning, designing, developing, and the evaulation of its current state. With evaulation complete, we would apply some corrections, then repeat the process. I want to see how professionals develop and approach projects in practice, not just in theory.

Amazon Review Predictor

Platform

Website

Language

Python

Released

Winter 2017

Updated

Spring 2017

Summary

   This was a finished school project that I was lucky enough to work on. It gave me a practical outlet to experiment with machine learning.

   The algorithm takes components of a standard Amazon review, i.e. user name, review text, date posted, and then predicts what category the review belongs to, e.g. Pets, Books, or even Music. Using an ensemble of algorithms we were able to achieve an ~85% success rate on 57,000 reviews. The ensemble was comprised of 5 algorithms: Multinomial Multinomial Naïve Bayes, Bernoulli Naïve Bayes, Logistic Regression, Random Forests, and Gradient Boosting.
